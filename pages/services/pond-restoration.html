When Do You Need Restoration?

Pond restoration is recommended when:

  • Your pond hasn't been cleaned in several years
  • Water is extremely murky or has persistent algae blooms
  • There's a thick layer of sludge on the bottom
  • Water quality issues persist despite regular maintenance
  • You're experiencing recurring problems with clarity or odor
  • You want a "fresh start" for your pond

Our Restoration Process

  1. Assessment: We evaluate your pond's condition and create a restoration plan.
  2. Fish & Plant Care: We safely relocate fish and protect plants during the process.
  3. Drain & Clean: Complete removal of water, sludge, and debris.
  4. Surface Cleaning: Scrubbing and cleaning all pond surfaces.
  5. System Maintenance: Deep cleaning of filters, pumps, and equipment.
  6. Water Treatment: Refilling with properly treated and balanced water.
  7. Restart: Restarting equipment and reintroducing fish.
  8. Follow-up: Post-restoration care recommendations and check-in.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a restoration take?

Most pond restorations take a full day, depending on pond size and condition. We'll give you a timeline when we assess your pond.

Will restoration harm my fish?

No. We take great care to protect your fish during restoration. We use temporary holding tanks with proper aeration and water quality, and we reintroduce them carefully after the pond is ready.

How often should I do a full restoration?

With regular maintenance, most ponds only need a full restoration every 3-5 years. However, if you've neglected maintenance, a restoration can get you back on track.
